Chances are that if you've been alive anytime in the last 24 years, you've heard of Pokémon.

But even if you haven't, I'm here to give you a rundown.

History

Pokémon (or Pocket Monsters) is a video game series created in 1996 by the company Game Freak, published by Nintendo.

Since it's inception, the Pokémon series has gone on to spawn over 120 games, along with several books, movies, comics, toys, and more.

How To Play

A main series Pokémon game is a turn-based RPG, with the player going around and collecting Pokémon to battle with so they can become champion of the Pokémon League.

In essence, Pokémon battles are similar to rock-paper-scissors. Every Pokémon and Move has a type (or types), that they are strong and weak against.

For Example:

  • If the Fire-Type Pokemon Charmander is attacked with a water move, the move will be 'super effective!' and deal double damage. If it is hit with a grass move, the move will be 'not very effective!'
  • If the Water-Type Pokemon Squirtle is attacked with a grass move, the move will be 'super effective!' and deal double damage. If it is hit with a fire move, the move will be 'not very effective!'
  • If the Grass-Type Pokemon Bulbasaur is attacked with a fire move, the move be 'super effective!' and deal double damage. If it is hit with a water move, the move will be 'not very effective!'

There are a total 18 Pokemon-types, each with their own strengths and weaknesses, and every Pokémon has different abilities, stats

and moves it can use, making every Pokémon battle a much more strategic game then you'd think!
